Burning Crop Residues

< Back


Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions from burning crop residues consist of methane and nitrous oxide gases produced by the combustion of a percentage of the crop residues burnt on-site. Computed at Tier 1 following the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National Greenhouse Gas Inventories, Vol. 4, Ch. 2 and 5; available by country, with global coverage and relative to the period 1990-2010, with annual updates.
